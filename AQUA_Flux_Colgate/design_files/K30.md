## Issues Observed

### SDA Line Held Low (I2C Hanging)

This is a frequent K30 occurrance that is caused by its limited processor and software-implemented I2C communications.  The only solution is to handle K30's limitations through the interface software, such as reattempting communication and in the worst-case "bit banging" the I2C bus to reset communications.

These events will appear in the logs as "I2C reset" events. This is an expected failure from which the software will recover.

### Spurious I2C Addresses Reported by K30

The most common cause is that the **SDA line is being held low by the sensor**, which makes it appear to ACK every address the scanner tries. The I2C scanner interprets any pulled-down SDA during the ACK slot as a device responding. This can happen because:

- **The sensor crashed mid-transaction** — if power was interrupted or the bus was reset while the K30 was in the middle of clocking out a byte, its internal state machine can get stuck holding SDA low.
- **The sensor's I2C peripheral locked up** — some microcontrollers/sensors have a known bug where the I2C peripheral hangs and drives SDA low indefinitely.
- **Damaged I2C output driver** — the SDA pin's open-drain FET may have failed shorted to GND, permanently pulling the line low regardless of bus activity.
- **Firmware corruption inside the K30** — the sensor runs its own firmware. A power glitch during operation can corrupt internal state and cause erratic bus behavior.
- **Wiring fault coincidentally on SDA** — a solder bridge or damaged wire pulling SDA to GND would produce the same symptom and isn't the sensor's fault at all.
- **Decoupling/power supply issue** — insufficient bypass capacitance or a noisy supply can cause the K30's MCU to behave erratically, including driving the bus incorrectly.

The SenseAir K30 specifically has a known sensitivity to **brown-out conditions**. If a full power cycle doesn't fix it, the sensor's I2C interface may be permanently damaged and it would need to be replaced, as the K30 doesn't expose a way to reflash or reset its firmware externally.

## Troubleshooting Procedures

**Scope or logic-analyze SDA at idle** — if SDA is sitting at logic low instead of being pulled high by the pull-up resistors, the line is stuck.

**Power cycle the K30 with a full 10+ second off period** — the CO2 sensor takes time to fully discharge. Some I2C lockups clear on cold restart.
